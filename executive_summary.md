1) Project Overview:
This analysis explores transactional data from an online retail business to understand revenue patterns, customer behavior, and retention challenges. The goal is to identify key drivers of revenue and opportunities to improve customer retention.

2) Key Findings:
 1. United Kingdom contributes the majority of revenue, followed by several European countries such as Germany and France.
 2. Decorative household items and gift products dominate the top-selling product list.
 3. Approximately 1.86% transactions are cancellations.

3) Revenue Pattern:
Revenue shows a clear seasonal trend, with peaks observed during the final months of the year. This suggests that the business heavily benefits from holiday demand. Outside peak periods, revenue remains relatively stable but lower in comparison.

4) Retention Problem:
Analysis of customer purchase behavior reveals that a large proportion of customers make only a single transaction. The repeat purchase rate is relatively low, indicating a retention challenge. This suggests that while customer acquisition is effective, customer retention strategies may need improvement.

5) Country-Level Differences:
Revenue distribution is highly skewed toward one primary country, with significantly lower contributions from other regions. This indicates strong dependence on a single market, presenting both an opportunity for expansion and a potential risk due to lack of diversification.

6) Business Recommendations:
  1. Focus marketing campaigns during peak seasonal months to maximize revenue.
  2. Implement customer retention strategies such as loyalty programs or personalized offers.
  3. Expand marketing efforts in underperforming countries to diversify revenue sources.
  4. Promote top-performing products through bundling or discounts.
  5. Encourage repeat purchases through targeted email campaigns.
